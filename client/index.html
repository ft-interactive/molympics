Thomas Burke (Athens 1896, 400m) Burke won the inaugural Olympic 400 metres final in 54.2 seconds. This might be slower than Mo Farah's 25th lap in 2012, but the comparison is a little unfair: Burke ran his 400m on a 333m track in Athens with "with bends so tight they necessitated sharp braking", according to IAAF historians. He had won a 400m race the previous year with a time of 49.6 seconds. Michael Johnson (Atlanta 1996, 400m) Johnson raced to gold in 43.49 seconds, which is still an Olympic record. Johnson is the fastest person ever over 400 metres - his world record of 43.18 seconds from 1999 also stands today. Steve Ovett (Moscow 1980, 800m) In this 2-lap race, a relatively slow opening lap of 54.6 seconds was followed by a final lap of 50.8 seconds, meaning Ovett's average pace over the 2 laps of the 800 metres was 52.7 seconds, the speed we've used here to compare with Mo Farah. Sebastian Coe (Los Angeles 1984, 1500m) Ovett's great rival, and now president of the IAAF, Coe set an Olympic record in Los Angeles with a time of 3m 32.53s, equivalent to 56.7 seconds per 400m. David Rudisha (London 2012, 800m) Rudisha's outstanding world record time of 1m 40.91s means an average of 50.5 seconds per 400m. Marie-José Pérec (Atlanta 1996, 400m women) How do the fastest women compare? Pérec ran 400 metres in 48.25 seconds at Atlanta 1996, which is still an Olympic record - nearly 5 seconds behind Michael Johnson's Olympic record, but considerably faster than Burke's time from a century earlier. Pamela Jelimo (Beijing 2008, 800m women) In the women's 2-lap event in Beijing, 18-year-old Jelimo's average lap speed was a remarkable 57.44 seconds. Bradley Wiggins (Beijing 2008, cycling – 4,000m pursuit) Steve Jobs was a notable fan of the bicycle's ability to boost human performance: Wiggins' Olympic record of 4m 15.031s for the 4,000m pursuit means he covered 400m in around 25.5 seconds. Sun Yang (London 2012, swimming – 400m freestyle) Swimming is a slow activity, at least for humans - phenomenal as Sun Yang's Olympic record of 3m 40s is, it translates poorly onto the track. Racehorse – Winning Brew (2008) The world's fastest racehorse over 2 furlongs, Winning Brew's time for 400m is an electrifying 20.5 seconds. Greyhound – Brett Lee (2001) Not to be confused with his namesake, the Australian cricketer, Brett Lee is the fastest greyhound over 515 metres, a recorded speed that means 400 metres would take just 22.45 seconds. Tyrannosaurus Rex (Late Cretaceous) Research by Sellers and Manning (2007) suggests that T-Rex was capable of running 400 metres in around 50 seconds.
